## What is the T.A.C.O. Score?

The **T.A.C.O. Score** (Trump Always Chickens Out) is a 0–100 index that measures the frequency of presidential policy reversals relative to total policy actions.

<Info>
  **Higher score = more reversals.** A score of 91 means 91% of tracked policy threats were walked back.
</Info>

The term was coined by Wall Street traders who noticed a pattern: bold threats, followed by negotiated retreats. They built trading strategies around it. We built the meter.

---

## Score Components

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **TACO Count** | Number of documented policy reversals in the tracked period |
| **Holds Count** | Number of policies actually followed through |
| **Events (90d)** | Total tracked policy events in the last 90 days |
| **Reversal Rate** | `taco_count / (taco_count + holds_count)` × 100 |
| **TACO Score** | Weighted combination of reversal rate + recency |

## What Counts as a Reversal?

A policy action is marked as **TACO** when:

1. An official policy position, threat, or executive order is announced
2. Within 90 days, the position is publicly reversed, softened, delayed indefinitely, or abandoned
3. The reversal is documented by at least two major news sources

**Examples of TAC reversals tracked:**
- Tariff threats announced then withdrawn during negotiations
- Executive orders signed but immediately enjoined and not appealed
- Official statements retracted within 48 hours
- Policy positions reversed after market reactions

## Score Updates

The T.A.C.O. Score is updated:
- **Hourly** — when new events are logged
- **Daily** — full recalculation at midnight EST
- **On demand** — via our API (`/api/score`)

The score displayed on the live gauge reflects the most recent stored calculation. A live indicator shows when it was last updated.
